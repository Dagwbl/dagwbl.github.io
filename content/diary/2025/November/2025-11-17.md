## Notes from Gemini

Here are notes summarizing the key points and phrases from the video "How to Give a Presentation - Business English."
### 1. Introducing Yourself and Your Topic

You need to establish who you are and what you are talking about immediately. Adjust your language based on the formality of the situation.
 * Introducing Yourself:
   * Formal: "Good morning everyone. For those who don't know me, my name is [Name] and I work in [Department]." or "Before we begin, let me introduce myself briefly. I'm [Name]..." [01:01]
   * Informal: "Hi guys, if you don't know me, I'm [Name]..." or "I see some new faces so I'll introduce myself first." [01:18]
 * Introducing the Topic:
   * Simple Topic: "Today I'm going to be talking about..." or "I'd like to talk to you today about..." [01:45]
   * Complex Topic (Roadmap): Break it down into stages. "I'll begin by outlining... and then I'll go on to highlight... Finally, I'll briefly discuss..." [02:10]
### 2. Engaging the Audience
Good speakers work hard to earn attention; bad speakers assume they already have it. Use these three techniques to hook your audience early on:
 * Establish a Problem & Solution: Connect a boring topic to the audience's pain points.
   * Example: "Have you ever felt unfairly treated at work? ... We've been working on new policies to ensure fair recognition." [05:22]
 * Mention a Surprising Fact or Statistic: Grab attention with data.
   * Example: "Did you know that the average office worker only does four hours of productive work a day?" [06:06]
 * Tell a Short Story: Stories add an emotional dimension.
   * Example: "I once met a young salesman..." [06:34]
### 3. Organizing Your Presentation (Signposting)
"Signposting" uses specific phrases to show the audience where you are in the presentation. It follows the rule: Tell them what youâ€™re going to say, say it, then tell them what you said. [07:44]
 * Moving to the next point:
   * "Next I'd like to talk about..."
   * "Let's move on and discuss..."
   * "At this point, I'd like to turn to..." [10:28]
 * Adding detail:
   * "Let me go into some more detail about..."
   * "Let's examine [topic] in more depth." [10:54]
 * Concluding/wrapping up:
   * "To wrap up, let's remind ourselves..."
   * "Let's review the key points from this session." [11:04]
### 4. Dealing with Problems
Things often go wrong. Use these phrases to recover smoothly without panicking.
 * If you lose your place / forget a word:
   * "Where was I?"
   * "So, what was I saying?"
   * "What's the word in English again?" (Look at your cue cards instead of staying silent). [13:15]
 * If you make a mistake:
   * "Let me rephrase that."
   * "Actually, what I meant to say is..."
   * "To clarify, I wanted to say that..." [13:51]
 * If you forgot to mention something important:
   * "Let me just add one more thing."
   * "Let me return to an earlier point briefly." [14:15]
### 5. Handling Difficult Questions
Use the "Three D's" strategy when you can't (or don't want to) answer a question immediately.
 * Delay: Give yourself time to research or think.
   * "I've allocated time for questions at the end..."
   * "I'm not in a position to answer that right now, but I'll get back to you later." [14:52]
 * Deflect: Pass the question to someone else.
   * "That's an interesting question. Before I answer, I'd like to know what's your take on this?" [15:20]
 * Dismiss: If the question is irrelevant.
   * "Thanks for your input, but I don't see how that's connected to what I'm saying."
   * "I don't mean to be blunt, but I don't think that's relevant to today's discussion." [15:49]
